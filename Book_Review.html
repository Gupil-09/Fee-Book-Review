1. Harry Potter And The Philosopher's Stone

Author : J.K. Rowling

Review :

Harry Potter and the Philosopher's stone by J.K. Rowling is her Mona Lisa; her Symphony #7; her Citizen Kane. This book introduces readers to the magical world of Hogwarts and follows the journey of a young boy named Harry Potter as he discovers his true identity and destiny. As a book that has become a household name, it is worth delving deeper into what makes Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone such a beloved and timeless classic. The story begins with the introduction of Harry, an orphan living with his cruel relatives, the Dursleys. However, his life takes a drastic turn when he receives a letter informing him that he has been accepted to Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry. As Harry delves into the world of magic, he discovers that he is a famous wizard, known for defeating the dark wizard, Lord Voldemort, as a baby. Along with his new friends, Ron Weasley and Hermione Granger, Harry embarks on a quest to find the elusive Philosopher's Stone, which can grant immortality to its owner. The trio faces numerous challenges and obstacles as they try to protect the stone from falling into the wrong hands. One of the most compelling aspects of this book is the world-building done by Rowling. With intricate details and a rich history, she brings to life a world that readers can easily get lost in. From the magical creatures to the spells and potions, every aspect of the wizarding world feels real and believable. It is this attention to detail that makes the story and its characters so captivating and relatable. As readers follow Harry's journey, they are also introduced to the diverse and lovable characters who add depth and humour to the story. Another notable aspect of the book is the writing style of Rowling. Her use of vivid and descriptive language paints a vivid picture in the reader's mind, making it easy to immerse oneself in the story. The pacing of the book is also well-crafted, with a perfect balance between action-packed scenes and slower moments that allow readers to catch their breath and get to know the characters better. Rowling's writing style is both engaging and entertaining, making the book a page-turner from start to finish. One of the strongest points of the book is the underlying themes of love, friendship, and the battle between good and evil. Through Harry's relationships with his friends, Dumbledore, and even his enemies, readers are reminded of the power of love and the importance of standing up for what is right. The book also touches upon deeper issues such as prejudice and discrimination, making it a thought-provoking read. In conclusion, Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone is a timeless classic that has stood the test of time. With its well-crafted plot, detailed world-building, and relatable characters, it is no wonder that this book has become a phenomenon. Whether you are a fan of fantasy or simply looking for a captivating read, this book is a must-read for all book lovers. It is a story that will stay with you long after you have turned the last page.

Rating : 4.7/5

2. Harry Potter And The Chamber Of Secrets

Author : J.K. Rowling

Review :

The second book in the Harry Potter series contains all of the exciting ingredients which made the first one such a success. Harry’s cruel treatment by the Dursley family continues, as does the kindness and support offered by Ron Weasley’s family. The fantasy elements also abound, with the flying sequences arguably even more dramatic and fascinating than in Philosophers Stone. In the latter it was flying broomsticks, in Chamber of Secrets it is a flying car – a Ford Anglia belonging to Ron’s dad. The flying car is an inspirational inclusion which many children will relate to. How many of us have clutched the steering wheel of the family car imagining we are flying an aeroplane? The flying Anglia reminded me of another great fictional creation in the 50’s comic ‘Eagle’ – ‘Dan Dare Pilot of the Future’. When Dan finally makes it to the planet Venus, he finds ‘flying chairs’ as the internal transport system in one of the planet’s biggest cities – Mekonta. The plot is similar to the first book – the discovery of a lethal force concealed in the Hogwarts building. It presents a serious threat to staff and pupils alike - ultimately, the story revolves around Harry’s bravery in eliminating the menace. It leads to many more characters and developments, many of them quite frightening. The chamber is found to be the home of a basilisk – a giant snake with the power to kill people if they look at its reflection. Harry and Ron also encounter giant spiders reminiscent of the giant Martian war machines in H G Wells’sci-fi novel ‘The War of the Worlds’. Some believe that children may find such aggressive monsters too frightening. I would argue that children should be aware of life’s more confronting areas – and the Harry Potter novels ‘Harry Potter And The Chamber of Secrets’ also continues to address several more social and behavioural issues - Harry still suffers bullying by the Dursleys and the persecution of pupils who do not come from all magical families, is an obvious protest against racism. The dangers of acting upon unreliable information is another of the story’s messages. At a deeper psychological level, the narrative suggests that one’s character is the result of one’s choices – which overlooks the fact that choices are often made for you by circumstances. As usual, there is plenty to digest in Harry Potter. Above all ‘Harry Potter And The Chamber of Secrets’ is a top story full of imaginative and intriguing scenarios. It is as original and stimulating as the first – a pattern which I am assured continues throughout the whole series. I look forward to more to come

Rating : 4.4/5

3. Harry Potter And The Prisoner Of Azkaban

Author : J.K. Rowling

Review :

I just finished reading "Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban" and I'm still reeling from the experience. J.K. Rowling's third installment in the Harry Potter series is, without a doubt, one of the most captivating and thrilling books I've ever had the pleasure of reading. The story is a masterclass in storytelling, weaving together complex plot threads, memorable characters, and stunning magical world-building. The introduction of Sirius Black, a wizard with a mysterious past, adds a layer of depth to the story that will keep you guessing until the very end. The pacing is expertly handled, with moments of high-stakes action and suspense balanced beautifully with moments of quiet reflection and character development. The themes of friendship, loyalty, and the power of love are woven throughout the narrative, making this book a truly unforgettable read. The writing is, as always, superb. Rowling's prose is evocative and immersive, transporting you to the world of Hogwarts with ease. The characters are richly drawn and complex, with even the minor players leaving a lasting impression. If you're a fan of the series, you won't want to miss this installment. If you're new to Harry Potter, this book is an excellent introduction to the magical world that has captured the hearts of millions. Overall, "Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban" is a must-read for anyone who loves fantasy, adventure, or simply a good story well-told.

Rating : 4.6/5

4. Harry Potter And The Goblet Of Fire

Author : J.K. Rowling

Review :

Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire is another classic in the franchise. Personally, I find it to be (in the best way possible) one of the more average novels in the series. The novel, for the most part, has amazing pacing. The opening is tremendously exciting, hyping the Quidditch World Cup up from the beginning. Of course, the quidditch match is perfectly paid off with the best written match of the series. Fast forwarding a bit, from the minute the Goblet of Fire itself is introduced, the novel continues with flawless pacing. Many new characters are introduced and I genuinely think all of them are amazing additions to the series (except for Winky who I thought was a bit overdone), Ludo Bagman was funny but had a good twist, Krum was entertaining, Fleur was interesting and more. I think just about every character was written to their full potential and I mean that honestly. The only problem is everything between the Quidditch World Cup and the Goblet of Fire spitting out the Triwizard Tournament contestants. The Death Eater chapter following the quidditch match was a bit rushed, all over the place and poorly written at times and I can understand why Rowling had such a hard time writing it because it was a difficult task, but just because it's difficult does not mean it's good. After that, the pacing becomes too slow for my taste and really struggles to keep the momentum and interest that the other parts of the novel do so flawlessly. Being a Harry Potter fan I love Quidditch. Quidditch is a sport in the wizarding world involving a lot of excitement and thrill. Harry Potter and the Goblet of Fire has the most humor and suspense in any book I've ever read. And also, it is the book with the Quidditch World Cup. The most thrilling things happened in this book, involving The Triwizard Tournament and the rising of Lord Voldemort. Besides, it is the first book that ever mentions the existence of Albus Dumbledore's pensieve. I would recommend this book to ages eight to twelve, even though it is definitely suitable for all ages. It is a book that would make you feel you couldn't put it down. A super read for all ages. The words are so sweet.I love all the harry Potter series very much.Once we start reading we can't stop to read because i was so eager to know what is the next situation.I recommend this book with a pleasure . The last part of the book when Cedric gets killed by Pettigrew was really sad because he was really brave , but I loved the book a lot it was amazing

Rating : 4.6/5

6. Harry Potter And The Half-Blood Prince

Author : J.K. Rowling

Review :

I just finished reading "Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince" by J.K. Rowling and I'm still reeling from the experience. The sixth book in the series is just as captivating as the others, but with a darker and more mature tone that sets it apart. The story is expertly woven, with intricate plot twists and character developments that keep you on the edge of your seat. The return of a mysterious potions textbook and a mysterious past student of Hogwarts sets Harry on a journey to uncover the truth about his own destiny, and it's not long before he's entangled in a web of danger and deceit. One of the standout aspects of this book is the way Rowling explores the complexities of teenage relationships. The romantic entanglements between Harry, Ron, and Hermione are relatable and heartbreaking, making it easy to become invested in their lives. Meanwhile, the mentorship between Harry and Dumbledore is both poignant and powerful. The themes of love, loss, and sacrifice are woven throughout the narrative, making for a rich and emotionally resonant reading experience. The writing is as evocative as ever, with vivid descriptions of Hogwarts and its magical world that transported me back to my own childhood fantasies. If I have any criticisms, it's that the pacing can be a bit uneven at times. Some sections feel a bit slow or predictable, while others are fast-paced and thrilling. However, these moments are few and far between, and overall, the book is a gripping and emotional ride that left me eager for the next installment. Overall, I highly recommend "Harry Potter and the Half-Blood Prince" to anyone who's been following the series from the beginning. It's a masterclass in storytelling, character development, and world-building that will leave you breathless and craving more.

Rating : 4.8/5

8. Harry Potter And The Cursed Child

Author : J.K. Rowling

Review :

Harry Potter and the Cursed Child is a two parts theatre play which we were super excited to see. The special effects were fantastic, we were amused watching Albus, Scorpius and Delphi's transformations, it was a great fun. Part One was so much better than part Two. It was more dynamic, entertaining and intriguing. The dementors at the end of the part One gave us shivers and we were looking forward to part Two. We went for lunch to Pizza Express and came back to our seats full of excitement. But unfortunately the second part was not great then we expected. The relationships between Albus and Scorpius are not very clear, are they best friends or gay couple? I couldn't understand. Love was mentioned and there were hugs and hands were hold but yet in the final act Scorpius was interested in Rose...Please make it clear, I am confused. In the middle of the second part my 10 year old started yawning and almost felt asleep. Myself I felt uncomfortable watching the characters arguing and shouting and yelling at each other for a good half an hour and all stage play felt like a Soap Opera in the Wizarding World. All this drama about being bad immature parents blah blah blah, fake sobbing and mood swings made me really sad and I wanted to leave. The script of the second part is horribly written, the characters are acting weird and it's hard to understand what is going on. Especially Delphi, suddenly she appeared to be Voldemort's daughter, brrr! What a stupid idea!

Rating : 3.5/5

9. Percy Jackson And The Lightning Thief

Author : Rick Riordan

Review :

The Lighting Thief is the pilot novel in the series revolving around the life of a regular 12-year old boy, Percy who finds out he is the son of a Greek god. From there, Percy, his friends Annabeth, Grover and his half-brother Tyson go on dangerous quests to stop a certain evil Titan from rising, by returning a lightening bolt to Zeus, the original wielder. Rick Riordan's sense of humor is absolutely phenomenal—and this is one of my favourite characteristics in this middle grade book. I'd recommend it for children above the age of eight, and I'm sure there aren't many who don't enjoy these light, fast paced reads! On the way he experiences betrayal, death and scary truths that don't always do him good, but this tale tells us that every one of us has a weak spot, and sometimes, even that can be your greatest strength. The lessons to learn from these stories are hidden in between the lines, and that is another wonderful characteristic that makes a great read! This story is fast-paced, so don't read it after a week if you've already started it. The first time I read this book, I lost my train of thought near the end and flew away with Grover's shoes so... don't do the same thing! The books are wonderfully written, in first person, and makes the perfect read for anyone who likes Harry Potter (if that's a bench mark.) Happy Reading!

Rating : 4.3/5
